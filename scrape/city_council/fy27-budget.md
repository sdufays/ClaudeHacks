City Manager Yi-An Huang submitted the proposed Fiscal Year 2027 operating budget to the Cambridge City Council on April 29, 2026. At just over $1 billion, it marks the first time in Cambridge history that the city's annual operating budget has crossed the billion-dollar threshold.

## Key line items

- **$51 million** for affordable housing programs, including subsidies for income-restricted units, support for the Cambridge Community Development Department's housing production pipeline, and continuation of the Affordable Housing Trust Fund.
- **$16 million** for homelessness services, expanding shelter capacity, street outreach, and transitional housing support in partnership with nonprofits including Y2Y and Cambridge Warming Center.
- **$5 million** for early childcare for children too young to qualify for the Universal Pre-K program (ages 0–2), targeting families with incomes below 80% of Area Median Income.
- Increases across the Cambridge Public Schools budget reflecting enrollment growth and negotiated salary adjustments.
- Capital funding for infrastructure maintenance, including road resurfacing and park improvements.

## Process and timeline

The city manager's office held a public briefing on April 29 immediately following submission. The City Council will conduct public hearings on the budget over the following weeks:

- **Tuesday, May 5, 2026 at 9:00 AM** — General budget overview hearing (City Hall Annex, Sullivan Chamber)
- **Wednesday, May 6, 2026 at 6:00 PM** — Cambridge Public Schools budget hearing (Cambridge Rindge and Latin School, Fitzgerald Auditorium)
- **Tuesday, May 12, 2026 at 9:00 AM** — Department-by-department review session

Cambridge residents may provide public comment at any of these hearings. Written comments may also be submitted to the City Clerk's office. The full budget document is available on the City of Cambridge website.

**Final adoption vote: Monday, June 1, 2026** (subject to revision by the City Council).

## Context

Cambridge's budget has grown steadily over the past decade as the city's tax base has expanded, driven largely by commercial real estate and biotech sector growth. The city does not levy a residential property tax increase for FY27. City Manager Huang emphasized that the budget prioritizes residents most affected by the region's high cost of living, particularly renters and families with young children.
