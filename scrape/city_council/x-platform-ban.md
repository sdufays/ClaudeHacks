The Cambridge City Council passed a policy order on March 3, 2026 directing all city departments and official city accounts to cease use of the social media platform X (formerly Twitter). The order passed 7–2, with Councilors McGovern and Toner dissenting.

## What the order requires

The policy order directs:

- All city departments, agencies, and offices to deactivate or archive their official X accounts within 60 days.
- The City Manager's communications office to develop alternative public communications strategies — including enhanced use of the city website, email list, and other platforms — to ensure residents continue to receive timely official information.
- A report to the council within 90 days on implementation and any gaps in public communications.

The order does not apply to individual councilors' personal or campaign accounts, only to accounts operated by city government.

## Reasons stated

Councilors supporting the ban cited several concerns about the platform under its current ownership:

- Changes to content moderation policies that they argued had allowed an increase in harassment, hate speech, and misinformation.
- Changes to verification systems that made it harder for residents to identify official government accounts versus impersonators.
- Concerns about the platform's alignment with federal political actors in ways they considered inconsistent with Cambridge's values.
- Data privacy questions related to how X handles government communications data.

Councilors McGovern and Toner argued the ban was symbolic and potentially counterproductive, noting that X remains a significant platform for breaking news and civic discourse, and that city accounts using the platform helps ensure accurate information reaches residents who rely on it.

## Context

Several other Massachusetts municipalities and public institutions had made similar decisions in the preceding months. Cambridge's city government had maintained active X accounts for departments including the Cambridge Police Department, the city's emergency management office, and the Mayor's office.
