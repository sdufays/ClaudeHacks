The Cambridge City Council held its inaugural meeting for the 2026–2027 term on January 5, 2026, at Cambridge City Hall. The nine newly elected councilors were sworn in, and the council voted to elect Councilor Sumbul Siddiqui as Mayor of Cambridge for the term.

## Council composition (2026–2027 term)

Cambridge's nine city councilors are elected at-large using ranked-choice voting (PR-STV). The 2026–2027 council is:

1. **Sumbul Siddiqui** (Mayor) — Incumbent; focus areas: housing, immigrant rights, youth services.
2. **Paul Toner** (Vice Mayor) — Returned; focus areas: public safety, small business.
3. **Burhan Azeem** — Returned; focus areas: housing production, technology policy.
4. **Alanna Mallon** — Returned; focus areas: schools, climate, transit.
5. **Patty Nolan** — Returned; focus areas: climate, open space, fiscal responsibility.
6. **Jivan Sobrinho-Wheeler** — Returned; focus areas: housing, labor, civil liberties.
7. **Marc McGovern** — Returned; focus areas: homelessness, mental health, public schools.
8. **Quinton Zondervan** — Returned; focus areas: climate, sustainability, housing.
9. **Rachel Simmons** — New councilor; focus areas: public safety, neighborhood stability, small business.

## Election of the Mayor

Under Cambridge's Plan E charter, the Mayor is chosen by the City Council from among its own members, rather than by direct election. Siddiqui was elected Mayor by a 7–2 vote, with McGovern and Simmons voting for an alternative candidate (Toner). Siddiqui had previously served as Mayor from 2020–2022.

The Mayor of Cambridge presides over City Council meetings and also serves as chair of the Cambridge School Committee by virtue of the office. The Mayor does not have veto power or executive authority — Cambridge operates under a council-manager form of government, with City Manager Yi-An Huang serving as the city's chief executive.

## Context

Cambridge's at-large electoral system means all nine councilors represent the entire city, with no geographic districts. This creates shared accountability for all city policy. Residents can contact any of the nine councilors on any issue. Councilor contact information and meeting schedules are available at cambridgema.gov.
