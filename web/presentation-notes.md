# Presentation notes — Civic Signal demo anchors

## 🔥 Co-anchor: Cambridge's first billion-dollar budget — FY27 (proposed Apr 29, 2026)

- City Manager Yi-An Huang submitted the first billion-dollar budget in Cambridge history
- Headline line items: $51M affordable housing, $16M homelessness, $5M for early childcare (kids too young for Universal Pre-K)
- Public hearings: Tues May 5 @ 9am, Wed May 6 @ 6pm (schools), Tues May 12 @ 9am
- Adoption vote: Mon June 1, 2026

**Why this is gold:** It's literally happening right now. Your "what you can do" sub-agent can output: "There's a public hearing on the FY27 budget Wednesday at 6pm. You can speak." That isn't a hypothetical action — it's two days away from the demo.

## Strong supporting items for the corpus

- **Cambridge Street upzoning** (Jan 27, 2026) — 6-3 vote, six-story buildings Inman Square → Lechmere. Direct follow-on to the Feb 2025 multifamily reform — perfect for showing personalization picks up the thread across time.
- **Sanctuary-city ordinance amendments** (Apr 28, 2026) — unanimous, bars federal immigration enforcement on city property. Different persona territory.
- **X (Twitter) ban for city departments** (Mar 3, 2026) — light, recognizable, judges will smile.
- **One-year multifamily zoning report** (Mar 26, 2026) — accountability angle: what actually got built?
- **Mayor Sumbul Siddiqui sworn in** (Jan 5, 2026) — useful for the sidebar's "your reps" panel.

## Caveats to address

### Election cycle reality

Next Cambridge municipal election is **Nov 3, 2026** — six months out — and no major ballot questions are confirmed for it yet. The big recent ballot moment was the **Nov 4, 2025** charter revision. So the "what can you vote on?" framing is thin in the immediate term.

Options:
- (a) Lean on "what can you attend / comment on" instead of "what can you vote on" for the demo.
- (b) Include the historical Nov 2025 charter question to demo the ballot-question flow even though it's past.
- (c) Both.

(The FY27 budget hearings above largely defuse this — the demo's headline call-to-action is "speak at the May 6 budget hearing," which is more concrete than any pending ballot question would be anyway.)
