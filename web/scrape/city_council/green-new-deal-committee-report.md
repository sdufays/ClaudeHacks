The Ordinance Committee presented its third annual implementation report on Cambridge's Green New Deal Ordinance on April 13, 2026. The report, covering calendar year 2025, details progress toward the city's goal of achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2050 with an interim target of 50% reduction below 2008 baseline by 2030.

## Current status of Cambridge emissions

According to the 2025 Cambridge Community Emissions Inventory:
- Total citywide emissions in 2025: 880,000 metric tons CO₂ equivalent — approximately 31% below the 2008 baseline.
- **Buildings** remain the largest emissions source at 64% of total, driven by heating fuel use in residential and commercial buildings.
- **Transportation** accounts for 29%, with personal vehicle trips being the dominant factor.
- Electricity emissions continue to decline as the New England grid adds more renewable generation.

## Key Green New Deal actions in 2025

**Buildings:**
- The Cambridge Building Energy Use Disclosure Ordinance (BEUDO) now covers all buildings over 25,000 square feet. 94% compliance rate in 2025.
- The city launched a Home Energy Efficiency Loan Program offering 0% financing for residential electrification (heat pumps, electric stoves, EV chargers). 312 loans approved in the first year, disproportionately concentrated in higher-income households — the program is being redesigned to better reach lower-income renters.
- Stretch building code amendments requiring all new construction to be all-electric took effect January 1, 2025.

**Transportation:**
- The city's EV charging network expanded from 48 to 127 public charging ports.
- The Cambridge Shuttle program (free bus service on targeted corridors) added the Alewife–Central Square route in June 2025.

## Progress toward 2030 target

At the current rate of emissions decline, Cambridge is tracking to reach approximately 43% below baseline by 2030 — somewhat short of the 50% target. The committee recommended accelerating the Home Energy Efficiency program redesign and exploring a commercial building retrofitting incentive fund to close the gap.

A public forum on the 2026 implementation priorities is scheduled for May 20, 2026 at the Community Learning Center.
