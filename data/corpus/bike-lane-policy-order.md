The Cambridge City Council passed a policy order on February 23, 2026 directing the City Manager to work with the Traffic, Parking, and Transportation (TP&T) Department to develop a detailed engineering design for protected bike lanes on Massachusetts Avenue from Central Square to Porter Square — approximately 1.5 miles — and to return a design proposal to the council within 120 days.

## Background

The stretch of Massachusetts Avenue between Central Square and Porter Square currently has painted sharrows (shared-lane markings) and intermittent bike lanes that are frequently blocked by delivery vehicles and parking. Cycling advocates have long identified this corridor as a significant gap in Cambridge's protected bike network, which includes protected lanes on Western Avenue, Cambridge Street, and portions of Concord Avenue.

The order was introduced by Councilors Azeem, Nolan, and Zondervan following a public forum in January 2026 where residents raised safety concerns. Cambridge has seen a significant increase in cycling activity, with the city's annual bike count showing a 34% increase in cyclists on Massachusetts Avenue over the past three years.

## What the order directs

- TP&T to study three design alternatives for the corridor:
  - **Option A:** Two-way protected bike lane on one side of the street.
  - **Option B:** One-way protected lanes on both sides, with parking removal.
  - **Option C:** Hybrid — protected lanes between intersections, shared-lane at intersections to preserve turn lanes.
- Analysis to include impacts on parking supply, traffic flow, emergency vehicle access, and loading zones for businesses.
- Mandatory community meetings in each affected neighborhood (Cambridgeport, Mid-Cambridge, Agassiz) before any design is finalized.

## Arguments and context

Supporters of the order cited safety data showing that cyclists on unprotected segments of Massachusetts Avenue are significantly more likely to be involved in crashes. They also noted that the corridor connects two major transit hubs (Central Square Red Line and Porter Square Red Line/Commuter Rail) and several Cambridge Public Schools.

Business owners along the corridor expressed concern about parking removal and delivery access. The TP&T study is expected to include economic impact modeling. A final design vote is anticipated in late 2026.
