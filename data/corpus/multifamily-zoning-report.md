The Housing Committee presented a report to the full Cambridge City Council on March 26, 2026, assessing outcomes of the February 2025 citywide multifamily zoning reform one year after its adoption. The reform had allowed multifamily housing by right within a half-mile of rapid transit stations, eliminating special permit requirements that had previously made residential construction near transit expensive and unpredictable.

## Key findings

**Permit activity increased significantly.** In the 12 months following adoption (February 2025–February 2026), Cambridge received 847 residential building permit applications for projects of three or more units, compared to 512 in the same period the prior year — a 65% increase. Projects near the Red Line and the Green Line Extension corridors accounted for the largest share of new activity.

**Projects are smaller than anticipated.** The average proposed project size is 14 units, not the large complexes some opponents feared. Most applications are for adaptive reuse of existing commercial or mixed-use buildings and small infill developments on underutilized lots.

**Affordability results are mixed.** Of the 847 applications, 312 triggered the city's 20% inclusionary zoning requirement (projects with more than 10 units), resulting in a pipeline of approximately 1,400 income-restricted units if all projects are built. However, per-unit construction costs have increased due to materials and labor market conditions, and some developers have sought variances on affordability requirements.

**Anti-displacement concerns persist.** The Central Square and Cambridgeport areas near the Red Line have seen the highest permit activity, with some longtime commercial tenants reporting increased pressure from landlords following the reform. The Community Development Department is monitoring displacement indicators but does not yet have a comprehensive assessment.

## Recommendations

The Housing Committee recommended:
- Continuing to monitor anti-displacement metrics and reporting back in one year.
- Extending the by-right multifamily zone from a half-mile to three-quarters of a mile from rapid transit stations in select corridors.
- Piloting a small commercial tenant stabilization program in Central Square.

A full council vote on the committee's recommendations is expected in May or June 2026.
